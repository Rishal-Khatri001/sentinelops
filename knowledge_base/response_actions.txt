Response recommendations in SentinelOps should be based on the overall risk profile and the false positive likelihood.

When the alert has high risk and low false positive evidence, appropriate actions include:
- escalate the alert
- force a password reset
- revoke active sessions or tokens
- investigate the new device
- review surrounding login activity

When the alert has moderate risk or mixed signals, appropriate actions include:
- investigate further before containment
- validate whether the user was traveling
- confirm whether a VPN was in use
- check device history
- review nearby authentication events

When the alert has strong benign indicators, appropriate actions include:
- monitor the account
- document the benign explanation
- close the alert as likely benign if confidence is high
- continue watching for repeated suspicious behavior

Response recommendations should always be grounded in the evidence collected during the investigation. High-risk actions are more appropriate when the alert includes a new device, suspicious IP, cross-border location change, and low false positive confidence.
