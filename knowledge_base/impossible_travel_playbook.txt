Impossible travel alerts occur when the same user account appears to authenticate from two geographically distant locations within an unrealistic time window.

In SentinelOps, an impossible travel alert is treated as higher risk when the following conditions are present:
- the second login occurs only a few minutes after the first login
- the location changes across countries or very distant regions
- the second login uses a new device
- the second login comes from a suspicious IP address
- there is no VPN indicator present

These conditions may suggest account compromise, credential theft, session misuse, or unauthorized access.

Common investigation questions:
- Did the user recently travel?
- Was a VPN in use?
- Is the second device known or previously trusted?
- Is the second IP associated with suspicious activity?
- Are there additional failed logins or unusual events around the alert?

A lower-confidence impossible travel alert may occur when:
- the second login used a known corporate VPN
- the second login used the same known device
- the browser and operating system match prior successful logins
- the location change can be explained by network routing behavior

A higher-confidence impossible travel alert should be escalated when:
- the second login uses a new device
- the location change is cross-border
- the IP appears suspicious
- there are no strong benign indicators
