Device context is important during identity investigations because a new or previously unseen device can increase the likelihood that an alert is malicious.

In SentinelOps, device risk is higher when:
- the current login uses a different device_id than the prior successful login
- the device appears for the first time for that user
- the login also involves a suspicious IP or unusual location
- the alert occurs within a short time window after a prior login

A known device lowers suspicion, especially when combined with:
- matching operating system
- matching browser
- known location or expected user behavior
- VPN usage that explains location differences

A new device does not always mean compromise, but it should raise analyst attention when paired with:
- cross-border logins
- suspicious IP address ranges
- repeated failed logins before success
- missing or weak false positive indicators

When SentinelOps detects a new device in an impossible travel scenario, the device risk score should increase and the investigation should consider containment actions if other high-risk indicators are present.
